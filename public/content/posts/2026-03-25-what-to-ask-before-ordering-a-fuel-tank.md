> A fuel storage tank is a 20+ year investment. The questions you ask before signing a Purchase Order have outsized impact on what shows up at your site, when, and what it costs to keep running. Here's the checklist we wish more buyers used.

## About the tank itself

- **What standard is the tank built to?** UL 142, UL 2085, STI Flameshield®, STI-P3, or ACT-100-U. The standard determines the construction, the labeling, and what your AHJ will accept.
- **Is the standard listing current?** Ask for the manufacturer's UL or STI listing certificate. Verify the standard edition matches what your AHJ has adopted.
- **Single-wall or double-wall?** If double-wall, what's the interstitial monitoring port configuration? Can you connect a leak sensor?
- **What's the primary shell material and thickness?** Carbon steel is standard. For some fluids you may need stainless or coated interior.
- **Is the secondary containment integral or separate?** Integral (double-wall) is preferred for most installations.
- **Multi-compartment options?** If you need to store multiple fuel types in one tank, confirm the manufacturer's compartment options for your size.

## About fittings and accessories

- **What fittings come standard?** Manhole, fill, vent, gauge, level switch — make sure the standard package matches your install drawing.
- **Are fittings UL or NFPA-compliant?** Off-spec fittings can fail an inspection even if the tank itself is listed.
- **Will the manufacturer install the fittings, or do you?** Factory-installed fittings ship faster than field-installed.
- **Is the tank pre-piped for a pump?** If you're spec'ing a pump-on-tank package, confirm what the manufacturer plumbs versus what your installer needs to do.
- **What about the ladder, guard, and platform?** Required by some configurations, optional on others.

## About code and inspection

- **Has your AHJ pre-approved this tank type for your site?** Don't assume.
- **What setbacks apply?** Distance to buildings, property lines, ignition sources.
- **Is overfill protection included?** Some manufacturers ship overfill protection; some assume the installer adds it.
- **Will the tank ship with documentation for the AHJ?** UL listing certificate, test reports, manufacturer's declaration. You'll need these for inspection.
- **Does the tank meet your state's specific requirements?** California has Title 19. Texas has TCEQ rules. Some states have unique add-ons.

## About delivery

- **What's the lead time from order to ship?** Get this in writing on the Sales Order.
- **Is freight included or quoted separately?** A 10,000-gallon tank can be a $2,000-$5,000 freight cost.
- **Who selects the carrier — manufacturer or buyer?** Buyer-selected gives you control. Manufacturer-selected may be faster but you're trusting their freight broker.
- **What size truck and equipment is needed at the delivery site?** Tank delivery often requires a tractor trailer; a crane may be needed for offload.
- **Is the delivery date a guarantee or an estimate?** Most manufacturer terms make delivery dates an estimate only — read the fine print.
- **What happens if delivery is delayed by you?** Most manufacturers charge storage fees if you're not ready when the tank is. Ours are 5% of order price per month — not unusual in the industry.

## About installation and site

- **Is the site prepared?** Concrete pad, access for the delivery truck, room for the crane if needed.
- **Are utility locates done?** Underground utilities at the install site need to be marked before any digging or pad work.
- **Who does the install — the manufacturer, your contractor, or someone else?** Most aboveground tank manufacturers do not install. Underground tank installers are specialized.
- **Is bollard protection required?** Required by many jurisdictions for aboveground tanks near vehicle traffic.

## About warranty and service

- **What warranty comes with the tank?** Most manufacturers offer 1 year. STI-listed tanks (Permatank®, Fireguard®, Flameshield®, ACT-100-U) may qualify for longer STI warranties — but you have to register them within a specific window after install.
- **What voids the warranty?** Common voids: storing the wrong product, modifying the tank, moving the tank after install, vandalism.
- **Who handles warranty claims — the manufacturer or the distributor?** Direct from the manufacturer is usually faster.
- **Is the warranty transferable if the property sells?** Most are not. If this matters for your project, ask.

## About payment and contracts

- **What's the deposit?** Common terms range from full payment at order to 50% deposit + 50% at shipment. Always full payment for some manufacturers.
- **How long is the quote valid?** Most quotes expire in 10-30 days due to steel price volatility.
- **What happens if you cancel after ordering?** Many manufacturers (including Hughes) do not accept cancellation after Sales Order is signed — you forfeit the order price. Read the cancellation terms before you sign.
- **What's the dispute resolution mechanism?** Most manufacturer terms specify their home state's courts. Read where you'd have to go if there's ever a problem.

## The summary

Most of these questions get glossed over in the rush to get a tank quoted and ordered. A good manufacturer will walk you through them proactively. A great one will catch issues before they become problems on your install date.
