Compliance Guide

Overview of UL, NFPA, EPA, and STI standards that apply to fuel storage tanks. Use this as a starting point — always verify requirements with your local Authority Having Jurisdiction.

⚠️

This guide is for general reference only. Requirements vary significantly by state and local jurisdiction. Always verify with your local Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ), fire marshal, and environmental regulator before purchasing or installing any fuel storage tank.

UL 142 UL 2085 EPA SPCC STI Standards NFPA 30
UL 142
Aboveground Steel Tanks — UL 142

UL 142 is the Underwriters Laboratories standard for aboveground steel tanks storing flammable and combustible liquids. It governs tank construction, materials, and testing requirements. The UL 142 label on a tank means it was built, tested, and listed to this standard.

NFPA 30 (Flammable and Combustible Liquids Code) and NFPA 30A (Motor Fuel Dispensing Facilities) reference UL 142 as an accepted construction standard. Most state and local fire codes adopt NFPA 30 by reference, making UL 142 a de facto requirement in most jurisdictions for aboveground fuel storage.

UL 142 covers both single-wall and double-wall tank construction in horizontal and vertical orientations. Every Hughes Tank UL 142 tank ships with the UL label and documentation for your inspector.

Key Points
  • Required by NFPA 30 for most aboveground fuel storage
  • Covers single-wall and double-wall construction
  • UL label must be on every listed tank
  • Applies to diesel, gasoline, and petroleum products
  • Required by most state and local fire codes

UL 2085
Fire-Rated Tanks — Fireguard®

UL 2085 is the Underwriters Laboratories standard for Protected Aboveground Tanks for Flammable and Combustible Liquids. It requires tanks to withstand a two-hour pool fire test and a ballistic impact test — significantly more stringent than the standard UL 142 requirement.

UL 2085 tanks are required by fire codes in locations where reduced setback distances apply under NFPA 30 Section 22.11, or where the Authority Having Jurisdiction mandates fire protection. Common applications include airports, military installations, hospitals, government facilities, and retail fuel stations in urban areas.

Hughes Tank Fireguard® is UL 2085 listed. Every tank ships with full UL documentation including the label and test records.

Key Points
  • 2-hour fire resistance required by many AHJs
  • Ballistic impact resistance also required
  • Enables reduced setback distances vs. UL 142
  • Mandatory at airports, military, hospitals in many states
  • Full UL documentation included with every tank

EPA 40 CFR 112
Spill Prevention — EPA SPCC

The EPA's Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) rule (40 CFR Part 112) applies to facilities with aboveground oil storage capacity greater than 1,320 gallons in containers of 55 gallons or more, or underground capacity exceeding 42,000 gallons. Most commercial fueling operations are covered.

SPCC-regulated facilities must prepare and implement an SPCC Plan — a written document that describes how the facility will prevent oil spills and respond if they occur. Secondary containment is a core requirement: containment must hold 110% of the largest tank's capacity.

Double-wall UL 142 tanks, Flameshield® tanks, and steel containment pans all satisfy the SPCC secondary containment requirement. Hughes Tank can help you select the right solution for your facility.

Key Points
  • Applies to most facilities over 1,320 gallons aboveground
  • Containment must hold 110% of largest tank
  • Double-wall tanks and containment pans both qualify
  • Written SPCC Plan required at covered facilities
  • PE certification required for most plans

STI
Steel Tank Institute — STI-P3, ACT-100, ACT-100-U

The Steel Tank Institute (STI) develops standards for steel storage tanks used in fuel storage applications. Three key STI standards are relevant to Hughes Tank products: STI-P3 (Flameshield®), ACT-100 (Permatank®), and ACT-100-U.

STI-P3 listed tanks (Flameshield®) have a two-hour fire rating and built-in secondary containment through an intumescent material between inner and outer steel walls. STI-P3 is widely accepted by AHJs as an alternative to UL 2085.

ACT-100 and ACT-100-U are STI standards for underground steel tanks with factory-applied fiberglass reinforced polyester (FRP) coating for corrosion protection, meeting EPA underground storage tank regulations.

Key Points
  • STI-P3: fire protection + secondary containment
  • ACT-100: underground tank with FRP coating
  • ACT-100-U: ethanol and biodiesel compatible
  • STI listing accepted by most regulatory agencies
  • Full documentation provided with every tank

NFPA 30
Flammable & Combustible Liquids Code

NFPA 30 (Flammable and Combustible Liquids Code) is the primary model code governing how flammable and combustible liquids are stored, handled, and used. It is adopted by most states and municipalities as the model fire code for fuel storage.

NFPA 30A covers motor fuel dispensing facilities and repair garages. If you operate a fleet fueling station or retail fuel station, NFPA 30A applies specifically to your installation.

Key NFPA 30 topics include setback distances from property lines and buildings, secondary containment, vent sizing, emergency vent requirements, and fire protection requirements. All Hughes Tank products are designed to meet NFPA 30 requirements.

Key Points
  • Adopted by most states as the model fire code
  • Governs setback distances for aboveground tanks
  • Section 22.11 covers fire-rated tank requirements
  • NFPA 30A for retail and fleet fueling stations
  • All Hughes Tank products meet NFPA 30
